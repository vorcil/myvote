About MyVote

We are breaking these barriers though MyVote - the first site in NZ that addresses all voting requirements.

Online Voting is more than utilising technology, it is about breaking down barriers.

Barriers such as:

  • Location;
  • Accessibility and mobility;
  • Time restraints; and
  • Knowledge of both process and politics.

We are breaking these barriers though MyVote - the first site in NZ that addresses all voting requirements. The core of MyVote is that it is a one stop shop – from digital enrolment to on the day voting with all the other parts in between – it covers everything any voter might need. Going digital removes the barrier of location. Over 1 million eligible NZ votes live abroad and this will allow New Zealanders abroad such as our Armed Forces to easily have their vote count.

By providing ubiquitous access from any internet enabled device, accessibility and mobility issues are addressed. MyVote ensures inclusivity of the greatest possible number of electors by adhering to W3C and Government standards. Locations such as Hospitals or care homes, and electors with access or mobility requirements will be able to complete any stage of the election process from any location with web access.

On the day voting is not always compatible with our modern working environment. We will allow both advance and on the day voting so those with a more restrictive calendar are not excluded. With key dates and reminder facility our busy electors should easily find the small amount of time required to register their choice.

A recurring theme in our research has pointed to a requirement for more visibility of fundamental information. Where the foundations of the political parties and personal position of electorates can be found and considered. MyVote will be a reliable neutral sources of basic profiles but also links to additional channels such as social media. With a clear visual approach we hope to lift the mist surrounding New Zealand Politics and electors options. MyVote - the future of voting is here.
